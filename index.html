Space Exploration Mini-Guide

Essential Space Facts

  • James Webb Space Telescope (JWST): Studies exoplanets, star formation, and the earliest galaxies with powerful infrared imaging.
  • Artemis Program: NASA aims to return astronauts to the Moon by 2026, with a focus on building a sustainable base near the lunar south pole.
  • Mars Sample Return: Perseverance rover has collected samples. NASA and ESA are planning the first mission to bring them back by the early 2030s.
  • Earth Observation: Satellites monitor climate change, natural disasters, and deforestation, helping us protect our home planet.
  • International Collaboration: Agencies worldwide (NASA, ESA, JAXA, Roscosmos, CSA) cooperate on the ISS and prepare for future Moon and Mars missions.

Upcoming Missions and Timelines

  1. 2025–2026: Artemis II → First crewed Artemis mission orbiting the Moon.
  2. 2026: Artemis III → Astronauts land at the lunar south pole.
  3. Early 2030s: Mars Sample Return → First attempt to bring Mars soil back to Earth.
  4. 2021–Present: JWST Discoveries → Revealing exoplanet atmospheres and distant galaxies.
  5. Ongoing: SpaceX Crew Flights → Regularly transporting astronauts to the ISS.
  6. 2024–Present: Ariane 6 → ESA’s heavy-lift launcher begins service.
